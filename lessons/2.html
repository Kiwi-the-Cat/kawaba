roots

a word in kawaba is made up of one or more roots. the first root in a word is the head root and carries the base meaning of the word. this can be modified by subsequent modifier roots, grouping to the left. For example, in the word {¤ |language} {ka|kind, variety} {wa|say, speak}, ka is the head root and wa is its modifier root. This forms the compound word meaning language, a kind of speaking. This compound word can then be modified by the root ba to form the name of the language {¤ |kawaba} {ka|kind, variety} {wa|say, speak} {ba|part, piece}, the language of parts.

compound modifiers

a compound word can itself be used as a modifier by placing a hyphen before the compound modifier in the word. This is pronounced as stress on the following syllable. For example, a linguist would be {¤ |linguist} {sa|person, someone} {-|compound modifier} {ka|kind, variety} {wa|say, speak}. It is generally advisable to limit the number of compound modifiers used in a word as using multiple creates ambiguity as to what is being modified.
