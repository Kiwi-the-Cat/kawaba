Lesson 2 - Grammar

Here is an overview of kawaba grammar:

  • Sentences in kawaba have the structure subject - verb - object.
  • Adjectives and adverbs come after the word that they modify.
  • Questions are marked using the word {nu|question marker}. This applies to polar questions AND open questions.
  • The imperative is formed using the marker {no|imperative marker}>.
  • There are two ways of nesting sentences - You can reference the subordinate clause (for example "I think this: creatures are good"), OR you can use the preposition {u|vague preposition} to link the sentences. (More on this later in the lesson).

Basic sentences

Let's get right into reading some kawaba! Use the table to figure out what these sentences mean. Hover over the words to see the meaning, and the star to see a translation of the whole sentence.

{★ |I want water.} {da |I/me} {ji |want} {gin.|water}​ ​ {★ |You see a creature.} {ga |you} {wi |see} {pun.|creature}​ ​ {★ |They love colour.} {la |they} {mo |love} {san.|colour} {★ |This contains cloth.} {je |this} {pi |contain} {lan.|cloth} ​ ​

Nice! Notice that in kawaba, words are not marked for number, so {pun|creature} could mean "a/the creature" or "creatures" depending on context.

Adverbs and adjectives

In kawaba, adverbs and adjectives come after the word they modify. Here are some more complex sentences using adverbs and adjectives:

{★ |The big creature thinks differently.} {pun |creature} {bo |big} {di |feel} {me.|different.}​ ​ {★ |Big groups easily control small groups.} {ton |group} {bo |big} {don |control} {jen |easy} {ton |group} {ko.|small}​ ​ {★ |I broke the glass thing.} {da |I/me} {gu |broke} {o |thing} {gan.|glass}

Questions and commands

To make a sentence into a polar question, end it with {nu|question marker}.

{★ | Do creatures speak?} {pun |creature} {wa |speak} {nu?|question marker} {★ |Do you know?} {ga |you} {gi |know} {nu?|question marker}

Questions using {le|what} are also marked using {nu|question marker}.
