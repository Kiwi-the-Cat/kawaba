Lesson 4 - Grammar

This lesson aims to cover the rest of kawaba grammar.

As covered in Lesson 2, the basic structure of a kawaba sentence is as follows:

{context |context} {na |context marker} {location |location} {ni | location marker} {time |time} {nu | temporal marker} {subject |subject} {ne | subject marker} {verb |verb} {no | object marker} {object |object}

Adjectives and Adverbs

Adjectives and adverbs follow the word they are modifying. The effect of an adjective or adverb the same as making a compound with that word, but you may want to separate the words for clarity. Here are some examples:

{★ | "A big house is cold"} {lin |house} {ke |big} {ne |subject marker} {wen|cold}​ ​ {★ | "Old people know many things"} {sa |person} {lenpu |old} {ne |subject marker} {gi |know} {no |object marker} {se |many}​ ​

Conjunctions

In kawaba, "e" "i" and "u" are conjunctions

When joining sentences, it is necessary to put a comma before the conjunction:
{★ | "I feel good, but I am tired"} {da |I/me} {ne |subject marker} {si|feel} {pe, |good} {u |but} {da |I/me} {ne |subject marker} {ci|want} {bun|sleep}

Conjunctions can also be used with verbs as so: {★ | "You always think differently and feel differently."} {gu|time} {ce |all} {nu | temporal marker} {ga |I/me} {ne |subject marker} {fi|think} {he |different} {e |and} {si|feel} {he |different}, where {fihe |think differently} {e |and} {sihe|feel differently} forms the whole verb in the sentence.

Conjunctions can also be used in noun phrases:
{★ | "You or your mother, don't break the camera!" (imperative)} {ga |you} {i |or} {sun|parent} {fun |female} {ga | you} {ne |subject marker} {lon|break} {pu |not/don't} {no |object marker} {hun|tool} {wan |picture} {nin!|imperative marker}
