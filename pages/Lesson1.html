kawaba

Lesson 1 - Sounds and Roots

Sounds

There are seventeen letters in the kawaba alphabet - twelve consonants and five vowels. The realisations of the sounds can vary greatly, as long as each is distinct. In kawaba, the first syllable of every word is stressed.

Consonants

All twelve consonants are pronounced as they would be in English except 'j', which is pronounced as the 'y' in 'yes'.

labial coronal dorsal
nasal m n
plosive p, b t, d k, g
fricative s
approximant w l j

Vowels

The five vowels are pronounced differently to how they would be in English, however these vowels can be found in other languages such as Spanish, Esperanto, or toki pona.

front back
close i u
mid e o
open a

Only lowercase letters are used to write kawaba. Capital letters are never used, even for names or at the beginning of sentences.

Roots

There are 120 single syllable roots in kawaba. All other words in the kawaba are created by combining these simple roots.

Basic Roots

The 60 basic roots of kawaba express the most fundemental concepts, found in all languages.
