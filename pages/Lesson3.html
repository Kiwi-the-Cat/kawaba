Lesson 3 - Compound Words

Creating basic compounds

To convey meaning beyond the base vocabulary, kawaba uses compound words. Compounds are head initial, meaning they derive their base meaning from the first word in the compound.

To make a compound, simply chain roots together! For example, {pun|"creature"} + {pin|"sky"} makes {punpin|"sky creature"}, a "sky creature" that may be a bird, an angel, a flying insect, or anything else that could be described as a "sky creature". Remember that in kawaba, any word can be any word class - compound words can be nouns, verbs, adjectives, or adverbs!

Now, using the root table, see if you can figure out what these compounds might mean:

{★ | "computer" (thinking machine)} {hun|"machine"} {fi| "think"}​ ​ {★ | "kill" (die cause)} {di|"cause"} {ta|"die"}​ ​ {★ | "mother" (female parent)} {sun|"parent"} {fun | "female"}​ ​ {★ |"language"(kind/type of saying)} {ka|"kind/type"} {wa |"say"}​ ​ {★ |"bright"(sun like)} {ma|"like/way/toaas"} {kin |"sun"}

Well done. The translations given are not absolute, and the meaning of a compound is always relative to the context it is used in! Thus, {ma|"like/way/as"} {kin|"sun"} could easily be "hot" or "big" (or any other characteristic of the sun) too!

More complex compounds

When you compound more than two words together, there is a default grouping that they follow. So, a {pun|"creature"} {be| "small"} {lin| "house"}, is a small house-creature, - a small creature that might live in a house- not a small-house creature, a creature that might have a small house.

To regroup parts of a compound, we use the particle {'o| "of"}. Thus, to say "small-house creature, we say {pun|"machine"} {'o| "think"} {be|small} {lin|house} : creature of small house.

Now it's your turn! Try to make compounds for the following English lexemes:

{★ father | sungun} {[hint]|a father is a male parent}​ ​ {★ doctor | sadun} {[hint]|a doctor is a person who deals with health} {★ meat | minban} {[hint]|meat is flesh that we eat}​ ​ {★ rain | ginpin} {[hint]|rain is water that comes from the sky}​ ​ {★ translate | son'okawa} {[hint]|to translate is to change the language}​ ​

Well done. Again, note that these compounds are not absolute, and any compound that is understood is valid!
