speaking in parts — wa uba

{¤ |kawaba} {ka|kind, variety} {wa|say, speak} {ba|part, piece} is a minimalist philosophical constructed language that attempts to express the entirety of the human experience through just 120 single syllable roots. with a simple phonology, limited lexicon, and oligosynthetic grammar, the language is designed to be as simple, neutral, and expressive as possible. the language's roots are made up of a reduced list of 50 semantic primes, a set of fundemental linguistic concepts that cannot be defined more simply, in addition to 50 common semantic molecules, more complex yet highly productive concepts that can be defined using semantic primes alone, as well as 20 roots used for grammatical purposes.
